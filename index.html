About

My name is Rachael and I am a qualified Reflexologist. I am trained in foot and hand reflexology and was trained by and a part of the International Institute of Reflexology using the original “Ingham Method”, created by our very own Eunice D. Ingham.

I first found reflexology after travelling through Asia. I went there to try to find answers to my problems - I had been through a lot of heart ache and I needed to see if there was any point in life.

Throughout my time in Asia I started understanding the art of energy work and eventually started to really feel a connection with my surroundings. Something inside me wanted to do more for others - to give back in some way - any way I could help others get through hard times, as I have. I knew using this new found love for energy work had a part to play.

When returning from my amazing adventure I wasn't sure what path to take. I enjoyed massage and researched different massage methods however I was also very interested in the traditional Chinese methods. So after some research I discovered Reflexology. The bright colours on the foot and hand charts and the clever way the whole body was mapped out of the feet and hands intrigued me. I had to know more!

After a long year of hard work, dedication and a few tears, I got my qualification! And thanks to my course I became focused and ready to pass on the knowledge and the skill of reflexology. I feel everyone should try it! Helping others to feel happier and more positive is very special and rewarding.

I aim to take Reflexology to as many places as possible. I aim to do and have done: Coffee mornings, one-to-one treatments, retreats, festivals, hen and stag do's... and anywhere the client and I feel will be beneficial for them.

I will bring my energy and love of Reflexology to your life. I am here to help. Thank you for visiting my website, I hope we cross paths sometime soon.

Love, Peace & Happiness,

What is Reflexology?

The basis of Reflexology has been recorded throughout history, going as far back as the time of the ancient Egyptians. The practise was passed through many doctors and was finally developed by the late, great Eunice Ingham. With the support of Dr. Joe Shelby and Dr Riley, she perfected the therapy we know today, her family are still training many therapists, and Reflexology its self has bloomed - there are many different reflexology techniques and many reflex treatments, working with specific conditions and systems.

Reflexologists work with the feet and hands to help fine tune your body, allowing all your bodily systems to work in harmony.

The definition of reflexology is: Reflexology is a science that deals with the principle that there are reflex areas in the feet and hands that correspond to all the glands, organs and parts of the body. Reflexology is a unique method of using the thumbs and fingers on these reflex areas.

Reflexology relieves stress and tension, improves blood supply and promotes the unblocking of nerve impulses and helps achieve homoeostasis.

Stress and tension is linked to an alarming amount of everyday conditions which will bring the body out of balance and in order to keep the body operating at peak perfection reflexologists unblock anything in the way to allow for the blood to reach every gland and organ, fixing any short circuits in the nerve system affected by stresses and tensions, allowing relaxation, improving blood flow and bring more oxygen-rich blood to every inch of your body - Homoeostasis is reached.

Reflexology could help treat a never ending list of conditions from stress and tension to insomnia, morning sickness to carpal tunnel syndrome, joint problems to drug addiction... Or you can just take some time out for you!

It really is a science and it really does work. It could help a never ending list of conditions, even if you are fit and healthy something like reflexology is good to do just because of how beneficial it is for your body.
